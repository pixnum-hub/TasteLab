    🚫 Indian Negative Pairing Guard
    Toggle combinations known to clash in Indian cooking — the AI will actively avoid them.
    ✕
    🥛 Milk + 🐟 Fish
    Ayurveda & traditional cooking consider this viruddha ahara — incompatible, may cause digestion issues.
    ✕
    🥛 Milk + 🍋 Sour Fruits
    Citrus or tamarind curdles milk proteins; avoided in classic Indian cooking.
    ✕
    🍯 Honey + 🔥 Cooking Heat
    Ayurveda warns that heated honey becomes toxic (ama). Use only as a raw drizzle or post-cook.
    ✕
    🧈 Ghee + 🍯 Honey (equal parts)
    Equal weight of ghee and honey is considered harmful in Ayurveda. Unequal ratios are fine.
    ✕
    🥣 Curd/Yogurt + 🧅 Onion
    Raw onion in yogurt raita is common but slow-cooked onion and curd together can create a heavy, sour combination traditional recipes avoid.
    ✕
    🥣 Curd + 🐟 Fish
    A classic viruddha combination — both are considered cold and heavy together. Avoided especially in Bengali and Ayurvedic traditions.
    ✕
    🫛 Radish + 🥛 Milk
    Radish with milk is an Ayurvedic incompatible pair said to disturb digestion significantly.
    ✕
    🍌 Banana + 🥛 Milk
    Widely debated — Ayurveda considers this combination to dull digestive fire (agni). Often avoided in cooked dishes.
    ✕
    🧀 Paneer + 🌱 Sesame Seeds
    Milk products with sesame is noted as a viruddha combination in classical Ayurvedic texts.
    ✕
    🟡 Mustard Oil + 🥛 Milk
    Pungent mustard oil and milk are considered an incompatible pairing in North Indian and Bengali traditions.
    ✕
    🫙 Tamarind + 🥛 Dairy
    Tamarind's high acidity causes dairy to split instantly — a technical and traditional pairing to avoid in gravies.
    ✕
    🍋 Lemon Juice + 🥛 Cream/Milk in Gravy
    Adding lemon to hot cream-based curries causes curdling — use kasuri methi or amchur for acidity instead.
    ✕
    🌱 Sprouts + Prolonged Heat
    Over-cooking sprouts destroys their nutritional value — traditional recipes either keep them raw in chaat or add at the very end.
    ✕
    🟡 Haldi Milk + Excess Black Pepper
    While small black pepper enhances turmeric absorption, too much overpowers the golden milk's soothing quality.
    ✕
    🥔 Potato + 🌿 Taro (Arbi)
    Two heavy starchy roots together make an excessively starchy and hard-to-digest combination, avoided in balanced Indian meals.
    ✕
    🥚 Eggs + 🥛 Milk (cooked together)
    Ayurvedic texts classify eggs and milk as viruddha when cooked together at high heat — French omelettes may not apply here but scrambled egg in curry sauce is flagged.
